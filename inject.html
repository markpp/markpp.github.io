Injection

Posted on Mon 23 January 2017 in projects

Brine injection is selected as the basis for a "quick and dirty" project where an adaptive method will help achieve a more optimal addition of salt in particular. The project's duration confined to 3-4 month, this means that it is necessary to clearly deliminate the problem and make wide use of "hand held" solutions in order to quickly test the method.

Process description

Brine is added to a wide range of meats before packaging in order to mature, tenderize and enhance flavor. The brine consists of a mix of water and salt, phosphate, nitrate and in some cases sugar or other types of flavoring. Salt has the effect of increases the solubility of proteins and thereby improves the meats ability to retain water. However, if the salt concentration gets too high, the effect is reversed and the muscle fibers shrink and proteins risk getting washed out. For the best results, the brine should be uniformly distributed in the meat. This can be achieved by administering the brine externally or by injecting it directly into the meat. The salt will then diffuse through out the meat and distribute evenly with time, through chemical processes. Salt is able to diffuse at the most 0.5 cm. per day, so if the brine needs to defuse over long distances, it becomes very time intensive and impractical for industrial application. The process can be helped along by treating the meat to mechanically. The standard practice for quickly curing meat is injection of brine using a grid of needles. When using current injection systems alone, the results are not always satisfactory and the meat is subjected to additional treatments both before and after the injection of brine. The meat's ability to absorb the brine can be improved by hammering before injection, this extends the muscle fibers, which increase room for brine and improves the distribution.

Another project from DMRI, "Optimeret produktion af forædlede kødprodukter - Accelereret saltning med multistiksprøjtning" investigates the possibilities for reducing the time needed to produce e.g. a ham, including the salting, using existing equipment, in under one day(7 hours).

Physiochemical parameters: - Salt concentration * Water retention increases with the concentration of salt until the concentration get too high and the effect reverses - Type of salt * NaCl is significantly better than KCl at improving water retention - Sugar concentration * Sugar has a similar effect to salt - Phosphate concentration * Phosphate increases the pH and water retention - pH * pH decides which ion, Na+ or Cl-, plays the primary role in increasing water retention * A pH value around 6 has shown to lead to optimal water retention for NaCl - Temperature * Low temperature contributes to better water retention, 4° being optimal * High temperature speeds up the diffusion of salt through the meat - Type of meat * the amount of protein and the ratios between protein types, e.g. intracellular(soluble) and extracellular(non-soluble), differs between cuts * The pH differs between cuts, e.g. pork shoulder has a much higher pH than loin

State-of-the-art

Brine injection: In the meat industry, brine is most commonly added using machines with multiple injection needles, the number of needles typically ranges from 40 to 500 with a spacing of 12-25 mm. Possible improvements

Here is a list of features that has the potential to improve speed, quality and knowledge of the product in general. Some of the features already exist in available machines.

  • Speed
    • Increased injection cadence
  • Quality
    • Adjustable injection depth
    • Flow monitoring
    • Finer injection patterns without needing more and tighter packed needles
    • Advanced injection patterns
    • Avoiding areas with bone or non muscle tissue
  • Knowledge

To text: - Injection into meat that contain bones, areas of fat or cartilage * Meat that contain bones will break needles and pockets of brine can form around the bone * Areas with concentrations of fat or other non-meat tissues should not be injected with brine - Achieving a uniform distribution of brine quickly * Multiple process steps and a lot of time is required to insure a satisfactory result * Individual and local differences in meat's abilities to absorb the brine are not taken into account
