Augmented Cellular Meat Production (ACMP)

Talking points

The robot cell is necessary because production line is maxed out. The cellular concept provides: - Multiple operation - Flexibility - High equipment utilization - Redundancy - Parallel scaling

CPU vs. GPU analogy:

The cellular concept gives us the flexibility of the CPU and the parallelism of the GPU

Or Ferrari vs. pickup truck

The Ferrari has a high top speed but breaks down often, whereas the pickup ...

The collaborative robot cell combines: - Strength of robot - Flexibility of human - Speeds up and ease transition to full automation

Investigate what works for: - Intention communication * Visualization - Proactive collaboration * Strategies - Learning * Self-learning * Continuous learning - (Safety) * Aura as an example

Find out by applying the technologies to some practical cases.

Reactive -> Proactive collaboration

The WP are organized in order to control risk

WP1 - Identifying interface which allows for all day use

W2 - Integration of robot into collaboration

Questions

What can we learn form other industries and what can they learn from us - If it can be done for this industry it can be done for everything else

Why do the robots need to be self-learing? - More adaptable - Lets us achieve behavior that is difficult to program - Continous improvement - Intense research activity and rate of improvement

Who are the leaders in reinforcement learning? - Google DeepMind(UK)(David Silver and many more), with their A3C algorithm    * With A3C a network estimate both a value function V(s) (how good a certain state is to be in) and a policy π(s) (a set of action probability outputs) * Known for conquering the complex game of Go -   OpenAI - University of Montreal(Yoshua Bengio) - UC Berkeley(Sergey Levine) - University of Massachusetts Amherst(Barto) - University of Alberta(Richard Sutton) - University of Toronto(Geoffrey Hinton) - New Tork University(Yann LeCun - Stanford - University of Waikato New Zealand(Tony Smith) - University of Cambridge

Types of reinforcement learning - Actor-Critic reinforcement learning   * The Critic(state value function) evaluate the consequences of the Actor's(controller) policies and provide feedback to the Actor -   Model based * Tries to model the environment e.i. learn the whole world - Model-free * Attempts to learn policies directly e.i. sovle the problem - What is a policy * A function that maps from state to action - Off-policy * Gather information from (partially) random moves * Evaluate states as if a greedy policy was used * Slowly reduce randomness - On-policy * Start with a simple soft policy * Sample state space with this policy * Improve policy * Suffers from local minima
